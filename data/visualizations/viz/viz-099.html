The Long Leverage: Private Sector Debt Accumulation Since 1950

Private sector debt as a share of GDP for eight major economies, tracking the multi-decade financial cycles that define modern capitalism. The United States shows a near-continuous build-up from 76% in 1950 to a peak of 236% in 2008. France and Canada have steadily climbed since the mid-1990s, while Japan has been deleveraging from its 1990s bubble peak.

Source: IMF Global Debt Database — Private Sector Debt, % of GDP (Privatedebt_all)
