Divergent Destinies: European Population Since 1990

Population indexed to 1991 = 100. Ireland and Spain grew through immigration; Eastern European nations -- Latvia, Bulgaria, Lithuania, Romania, Croatia -- lost a quarter or more of their people to emigration and low birth rates. Germany is indexed to its first reunified figure (1991).

Germany 1990 value reflects only the former West Germany; the 1991 figure is the first post-reunification count and is used as the index base.

Source: Eurostat -- Population on 1 January (demo_pjan)
